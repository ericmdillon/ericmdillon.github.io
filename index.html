About Me.

I’m an eccentric undergraduate scholar and researcher at Marshall University, pursing a B.S. in Computer Science and English. After graduating from Marshall, I intend on pursuing a graduate education in digital humanities, archival and information science, or game development and design.

One of my current projects investigates how gender bias affects professor reviews using data mining and natural language processing techniques. Rejecting the terms “man” and “woman” in favor of “masculine,” “feminine,” and “indeterminate,” my research integrates contemporary gender studies principles with data science to evaluate existing concerns under a new, computational light. Preliminary findings have been presented at DiHuCon and IEEE Integrated STEM Education Conferences.

I recently completed an internship with the Library of Appalachian Preaching in which I archived over 70 unique sermons preached at Temple Israel in Charleston, WV. Furthermore, I completed research investigating the success of cybersecurity training efforts on West Virginia K-12 teachers as well as developing a prototype to teach K-12 students cybersecurity tools. These experiences in archival, pedagogy, and educational technology have continued to develop my ability to produce interdisciplinary work and collaborate with numerous departments at my university.

Feel free to contact me at anytime. I’m always looking for internships, research and academic experiences, and other opportunities.
